# PourStock — Pitch Deck Outline

**Slide-by-Slide Structure for Investor Presentation**
*12-Slide Deck — March 2026*

---

## Deck Overview

Target audience: Seed-stage investors (angels, pre-seed funds, Nordic tech VCs).
Presentation length: 12–15 minutes.
Purpose: Convey the problem urgency, product differentiation, market opportunity, traction, and team credibility. Drive a follow-up meeting.

---

## Slide 1: Cover

**Title**: PourStock
**Sub-title**: AI-Powered Operations Platform for Hotels & Restaurants

**Key message**: Establish the company identity and product category immediately.

**Bullets**:
- "Unifying hotel and restaurant operations in real time"
- Stage: Seed
- Location: Denmark
- Live in production at Sønderborg Strand Hotel

**Suggested visual**: Clean product screenshot — the table planning floor plan view with live arrival timers. Show, don't tell, that this is a real operational tool.

---

## Slide 2: The Problem

**Title**: Hospitality Runs on Fragmented, Manual Systems

**Key message**: The pain is real, universal, and expensive. Every hotel in your portfolio experiences this.

**Bullets**:
- Dinner reservation PDFs printed at shift handover → wrong tables, missed dietaries, wasted service
- Beverage stock managed on spreadsheets → 8–12% of F&B revenue lost to untracked waste
- Housekeeping tasks assigned verbally → rooms not ready, guest complaints
- No real-time link between reception, restaurant, bar, and management

**Suggested visual**: Split-panel infographic. Left side: chaotic stack of tools (PMS icon, spreadsheet, printed paper, whiteboard). Right side: frustrated restaurant floor during service — table plan wrong, guest waiting. Use red colour language to signal cost/pain.

---

## Slide 3: The Market Opportunity

**Title**: A €4.2B Market That Mid-Market Hotels Cannot Access

**Key message**: The market is enormous, the mid-market is underserved, and the wedge is clear.

**Bullets**:
- TAM: €4.2B — European hospitality operations software (2025)
- SAM: €380M — Nordic + DACH mid-market hotel segment (20–200 rooms with restaurant)
- ~38,000 qualifying properties in target geography
- 68% of European mid-market hotels will adopt cloud operations software by 2027 (IDC)

**Suggested visual**: Three concentric circles (TAM/SAM/SOM) in PourStock brand colours. Annotate each ring with the number. A small map of Northern Europe with dots representing target market density.

---

## Slide 4: The Solution

**Title**: One Platform. Every Department. Real Time.

**Key message**: PourStock replaces the entire fragmented stack with a single tool designed for live hospitality operations.

**Bullets**:
- AI parses dinner reservation PDFs in < 2 minutes — replaces 30–45 minutes of manual work
- Real-time sync across all devices: reception, restaurant, bar, housekeeping see the same state
- Mobile-first design — works on tablets and phones during live service, not just office desktops
- Six operational modules in one subscription: Table Planning, Inventory, Reception, Housekeeping, Purchasing, Reports

**Suggested visual**: Product module map — a clean diagram showing the six modules arranged around a central "Real-Time Sync" hub. Each module has a small icon. No clutter. Alternatively, a side-by-side screenshot of the table plan before (blank PDF) and after (fully populated floor plan with timers).

1. PDF upload screen
2. Parsed data table showing extracted guest/room/course data
3. Completed floor plan with timers active

## Slide 7: Business Model

**Title**: Per-Hotel SaaS Subscription — Simple, Scalable, Sticky

**Key message**: The revenue model is straightforward, well-understood by investors, and aligned with how hotels budget.

**Bullets**:
- Per-hotel pricing, not per-user — hospitality teams rotate staff constantly
- Three tiers: Starter (~€149/mo), Professional (~€399/mo), Enterprise (custom)
- Additional revenue: AI usage overages, PMS/POS integration add-ons, onboarding services
- LTV:CAC target > 8:1 — high-margin SaaS with low churn in a relationship-driven market

**Suggested visual**: Pricing tier table (Starter / Professional / Enterprise) with included modules ticked for each. Below: a simple unit economics summary (ACV, Gross Margin %, Payback Period).

## Slide 8: Go-to-Market Strategy

**Bullets**:

**Suggested visual**: Map of Europe with a phased expansion overlay. Phase 1 = Denmark highlighted. Phase 2 = Nordics added. Phase 3 = DACH added. Arrow showing trajectory. Include logos of potential partnership channels (HORESTA, Mews marketplace).

## Slide 9: Competitive Landscape

**Title**: The Mid-Market Has No Credible Integrated Competitor

**Key message**: Enterprise players are too expensive and complex. Point solutions are too narrow. PourStock owns the integrated mid-market position.

**Bullets**:
- Enterprise PMS (Mews, Oracle Opera): €500–€2,000+/month, 6-week implementation, no F&B operations layer
- Restaurant reservation tools (SevenRooms, Resy): No hotel ops, no inventory, no housekeeping
- Inventory apps: No table planning, no real-time sync, no hotel context
- PourStock: Complete integrated platform at mid-market price, AI-native, mobile-first

**Suggested visual**: 2×2 competitive matrix. X-axis: "Breadth of operations coverage" (narrow → broad). Y-axis: "Accessible to mid-market" (enterprise-only → mid-market). PourStock sits top-right. Competitors placed in relevant quadrants. Clear white space in the top-right corner where PourStock lives alone.

## Slide 10: Technology & Moat

**Title**: Built for Scale — Production-Grade Engineering from Day One

**Key message**: The technical foundation is a real moat, not a story. 12 migration phases completed with zero downtime is evidence of engineering quality.

**Bullets**:
- AI document understanding with configurable per-hotel parser profiles — not a generic API call
- Full multi-tenant isolation (Row Level Security) — data never crosses hotel boundaries
- Real-time WebSocket architecture — all devices sync instantly without polling
- 12-phase migration program completed with zero production outage — de-risks scaling to hundreds of hotels

**Suggested visual**: Clean architecture diagram: Frontend (React/TypeScript) → Supabase (PostgreSQL + RLS) + Edge Functions → AI (Gemini). Highlight the "RLS Tenant Isolation" layer visually. Include a brief technical credibility callout: "12 migration phases. Zero downtime."

## Presentation Notes

- Keep total deck to 12 slides for timed pitches (12 min). Add back appendix slides for longer sessions or due diligence meetings.
- Slide 5 (Product Demo) can be replaced by a live product walkthrough if presenting with a laptop. This is almost always more compelling than screenshots.
- Prepare for the most common investor objections in the Nordic SaaS market:
  - "Why won't Mews or Protel just add this feature?" → Answer: They are targeting upmarket enterprise, not simplifying for mid-market. Their incentive is expansion, not accessibility.
  - "How do you get hotels to switch?" → Answer: The AI table planning is a standalone wedge feature with no switching cost for initial adoption. Hotels add modules over time.
  - "Is this a features company or a platform company?" → Answer: Platform. The real-time coordination layer between departments is the defensible product; individual modules are the entry points.
